p-aramid biosynthesis


vision

We are aiming for biosynthesis of p-aramid fibers. Due to their unique properties, these materials are valuable for a variety of applications. In particular, they are uniquely suited to the demands of inflatable modules and habitats--the Bigelow Space Module on the International Space Station is made of a Kevlar composite. The rigidity, toughness, and low density of these materials also make them best suited for balloon reinforcement.

Current production of Kevlar is entirely chemical-based. It is dangerous and costly due to the use of concentrated sulfuric acid to keep it in solution during synthesis and spinning. Our goal is to biologically produce p-aminobenzoic acid and polymerize it to manufacture cost-efficient and ecologically sound p-aramid Kevlar analogue.

overview

selecting our cells

S. cerevisiae is perhaps the best-characterized yeast. We decided to attempt production of p-ABA in S. cerevisiae due to the extent it has been studied as well as its presence in the Shikimate pathway. Using metabolic modelling tools, we will further optimize p-ABA production in our yeast strain.













modifying a metabolic pathway

The Shikimate pathway is primarily characterized by its end products: aromatic amino acids (phenylalanine, tyrosine). Synthesis of p-ABA occurs at a much lower rate as an intermediate for folate synthesis, and omission of p-ABA from the growth media significantly decreases the yeast's growth rate. However, regulation of the pathway through RNAi induced by quorum sensing helps to mitigate this issue.












polymerizing the product

p-ABA diffuses through the membrane of the individual S. cerevisiae into the media. By purifying the media, we are able to obtain pure p-ABA, which we polymerize to a p-aramid. We accomplish this through the use of a coupling agent to form amide bonds between the p-ABA monomers.




functionalizing p-aramid fibers

We will produce p-aramid fibers which are useful in a variety of applications. In the scope of our inflatables project, it is will be integrated into the membrane to produce a biosensing balloon or a semi-permanent inflatable module.
